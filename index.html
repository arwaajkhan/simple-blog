Top 10 places to visit in Pokhara

1. Lakeside

Lakeside is a popular tourist destination beside Phewa Tal (lake) in Nepal's second largest city Pokhara. The main areas of Pokhara are broken up into Lakeside, Damside and the old Pokhara. Within Lakeside there's now a north Lakeside and a South Lake side.
It's all quite easy to navigate as tourist buses drop most tourists off at the border between Lakeside and Damside. After that it's an easy walk or taxi to the start of south Lakeside and into the ongoing development of north Lakeside. For most people it's just "Lakeside" - which consists of a quiet main road that runs alongside Lake Phewa Tal in Pokhara.

2. Birauta Waterfall

Birauta Waterfall is a beautiful waterfall located in the Pokhara city of Nepal. It is situated in the Birauta area, which is about 15 minutes away from the main city area. The waterfall is surrounded by lush green forests, and the water cascades down from a height of approximately 100 feet.
The Birauta Waterfall is a popular tourist attraction in Pokhara and attracts many visitors throughout the year. It is an ideal place for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and photographers. Visitors can enjoy the natural beauty of the waterfall, take a refreshing swim in the cool waters, or simply relax in the serene surroundings.

3. Sarangkot

Sarangkot is a popular tourist destination located in the outskirts of Pokhara, a city in western Nepal. It is a hilltop village situated at an elevation of 1600 meters above sea level and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Himalayan mountain range, including Annapurna, Dhaulagiri, and Machhapuchhre (Fishtail).
Sarangkot is renowned for its stunning sunrise and sunset views, which attract tourists from all over the world. Visitors can also enjoy paragliding, trekking, and hiking in the surrounding hills and forests.
The village has a few guesthouses and restaurants that cater to tourists, and it is a popular starting point for treks to nearby destinations such as Dhampus, Ghandruk, and Annapurna Base Camp.

4. Tal Barahi

Tal Barahi, also known as Barahi Temple, is a popular Hindu temple located on a small island in Phewa Lake in Pokhara, Nepal. The temple is dedicated to the goddess Barahi, who is believed to be an incarnation of Hindu goddess Durga.
Tal Barahi is one of the most visited tourist destinations in Pokhara, and it can be reached by a short boat ride from the Lakeside area of Pokhara. The temple is surrounded by lush green forests, and the serene environment makes it a perfect place for meditation and relaxation.
Tal Barahi is an important cultural and religious site in Pokhara and attracts many local and international tourists throughout the year. It is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Pokhara and looking to experience the cultural and natural beauty of Nepal.

5. International Mountain Museum

The International Mountain Museum is a museum located in the city of Pokhara, Nepal, dedicated to the mountains of the world and the people who live in and around them. The museum was opened in 2002 with the objective of promoting and preserving the cultural and natural heritage of the mountain regions of Nepal and the world.
The International Mountain Museum has several exhibits that showcase the history, culture, and biodiversity of the Himalayan mountain range, as well as other mountain regions around the world. The exhibits include photographs, paintings, artifacts, and interactive displays that provide a comprehensive overview of the mountains and the people who inhabit them.

6. Fewa Powerhouse

Fewa Powerhouse is a hydroelectric power station located in Pokhara, Nepal. It is situated on the bank of Phewa Lake, the second-largest lake in Nepal, and generates electricity using the water from the lake.
The powerhouse was built in the 1950s with the help of the Austrian government, and it has a capacity of generating 1.4 MW of electricity. The electricity generated by the powerhouse is supplied to the national grid and is used to power homes, businesses, and industries in and around the Pokhara region.
The Fewa Powerhouse is a popular tourist attraction in Pokhara, and visitors can take a tour of the facility to learn about the process of generating electricity from water. The powerhouse also offers beautiful views of Phewa Lake and the surrounding hills, making it a popular spot for photography.

Mahendra Cave is a natural limestone cave located in the city of Pokhara, Nepal. The cave was discovered in the 1950s and was named after King Mahendra Bir Bikram Shah Dev, the former king of Nepal.
Mahendra Cave is a natural limestone cave located in the city of Pokhara, Nepal. The cave was discovered in the 1950s and was named after King Mahendra Bir Bikram Shah Dev, the former king of Nepal.
The cave is well-lit, and visitors can walk through the cave and observe the natural formations up close. There is also a small stream that flows through the cave, adding to its natural beauty. 8. Bat Cave

It is also known as Chamero Odhaar. The cave is located in the Batulechaur area of Pokhara, close to the famous Mahendra Cave.
As the name suggests, the cave is home to several species of bats, including the Indian flying fox and the Himalayan bat. Visitors can explore the cave and observe the bats up close, but it is recommended to take the help of a local guide for safety.
The Bat Cave is a unique destination for nature enthusiasts and those interested in exploring the natural wonders of Pokhara. It is also a popular spot for photography, as the cave offers unique views of the bats hanging from the cave's ceilings.

9. Birauta

Birauta is a residential area located in the city of Pokhara, Nepal. It is situated to the north of the Pokhara valley, near the famous Fewa Lake. Birauta is a rapidly developing area and is home to many shops, restaurants, and hotels.
Birauta is also known for its educational institutions, including the Gandaki Boarding School, one of the most prestigious boarding schools in Nepal, and the Pokhara University, a renowned university that offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs.
Birauta is well-connected to other parts of Pokhara, and visitors can easily access the area by public transportation or private vehicle. The area is surrounded by beautiful hills and offers stunning views of the Annapurna mountain range.

10. Bala Dada way View

Balam Dada is a small village located in the Kaski district of Pokhara, Nepal. The village is situated at an altitude of about 1,600 meters and is surrounded by lush green forests and hills.
Balam Dada is a popular destination for trekking and hiking enthusiasts, and it offers stunning views of the Annapurna mountain range and the surrounding landscape. Visitors can explore the village and interact with the locals to learn about their daily life and culture.
